# Consumer Motivation Analyzer

> A Claude AI skill for deep consumer purchase motivation analysis, based on 20+ years of brand marketing experience.

## 🎯 What This Skill Does

**"What consumers say isn't always what they truly want."**

This skill helps you uncover the real drivers behind consumer behavior by going beyond surface-level responses to reveal functional and psychological motivations.

## 🔍 The Three-Layer Analysis Framework

### Layer 1: Surface Needs (What They Say)
Conscious justifications like:
- "The brand is well-known"
- "My friend recommended it"
- "The price is right"

⚠️ These are often excuses or rationalizations, not true motivations.

### Layer 2: Functional Needs (What They Actually Need)
What the product actually solves:
- Save time
- Save money
- More convenient
- Better usability

### Layer 3: Psychological Needs (The Real Drivers) 🔑

| Psychological Need | Typical Behavior | Example |
|---------------------|------------------|---------|
| **Security** | Fear of being cheated | Buying big brands just to be safe |
| **Belonging** | Being part of a group | Celebrity同款, following trends |
| **Superiority** | Standing out from others | Limited editions, luxury brands |
| **Self-Identity** | "This is who I am" | Status-matching purchases |
| **Social Currency** | Something to share | Check-ins, unboxing content |
| **Compensation** | Making up for something | "Treating myself" spending |
| **Anxiety Relief** | Eliminating worries | Education, health products |

## 📊 Analysis Methods

### Method 1: The Five Whys
Keep asking "why" until you reach the core motivation:

```
User: "I bought this car because it has big space"
↓ Why is big space important to you?
User: "Because I often take my family out"
↓ Why is taking family out important to you?
User: "I want my kids to be happy"
↓ Why is making kids happy important to you?
User: "My parents never had time for me. I don't want to repeat that"
→ True Motivation: Compensation + Security (being a good parent)
```

### Method 2: Alternative Comparison
Ask: "If this product didn't exist, what would you buy instead? Why?"

The differences reveal real purchase reasons.

### Method 3: Extreme Hypothesis
Test with hypothetical extremes:
- "If only you knew about this product, would you still buy it?" → Tests social attributes
- "If this product cost you 1 extra hour daily, would you still buy?" → Tests true need intensity
- "If this product was ugly enough that nobody wanted to look at it, would you still buy?" → Tests face-value

### Method 4: Purchase Timing Analysis
When do they buy? Why at that time?

- Birthday/Holiday → Ritual, self-reward
- Promotion/Discount → Bargain hunting or FOMO?
- When needed → Real functional need
- Bad mood → Emotional compensation
